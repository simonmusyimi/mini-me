# Product Strategy

## Target User

V1 is built for Simon: an IT student at KCA University in Nairobi who is learning software engineering and AI, building Mini-Me in public, trying to create income through tech, and working on fitness and discipline.

## Pain Points

- Too many possible projects.
- Too much tool research.
- Not enough mentor feedback.
- Weak network.
- Limited money.
- High ambition but inconsistent execution.

## Week 1 Magic Moment

Simon runs `/plan` and feels: "This thing actually knows my situation and tells me what I should do next."

## Unfair Advantage

Mini-Me starts with a sharply defined first user. It does not need to serve everyone. It can become useful by learning Simon's real goals, risks, projects, and review history.

## Moat

The moat is personal memory plus daily feedback. Over time, Mini-Me should know Simon's patterns better than generic assistants do.

## V1 Success Metric

Simon uses Mini-Me for seven consecutive days to plan, complete tasks, and review outcomes.

## What Not To Build Yet

- Mobile app.
- Web dashboard.
- Chatbot UI.
- Browser automation.
- Notifications.
- Calendar integrations.
- Social integrations.
- Voice.
- Autonomous agents.
