# Vision

Mini-Me exists because ambitious people without mentors, money, or network need more than inspiration. They need a system that helps them decide what to do next, learn from what happened, and build momentum before opportunity arrives.

For Simon, Mini-Me starts as a tiny daily planner. Long term, it becomes a personal feedback loop that understands his goals, patterns, projects, constraints, and execution history. The dream is a digital twin that can challenge him, coach him, route work to specialist agents, and keep his ambition organized.

Emerging-market builders often face a gap that tools alone do not solve. They may have internet access, but not guidance. They may have ambition, but not a network. They may have ideas, but not a clean path from idea to shipped work. Mini-Me is meant to become infrastructure for that gap.

This is not just a productivity app. Productivity apps store tasks. Mini-Me should learn from outcomes. It should notice what Simon keeps avoiding, what produces progress, what distracts him, and what deserves his best hours.

"Infrastructure of ambition" means the support system underneath the person: memory, planning, reflection, accountability, and eventually useful agents. V1 is small because the loop matters more than the interface.
